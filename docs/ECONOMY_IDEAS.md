You are correct that eventually the game needs more money movement than just:

* puppy sales
* stud fees
* occasional dog sales

Otherwise the economy tends to collapse into either:

1. infinite money inflation, or
2. everyone becoming perpetually broke once realistic costs exist.

The important part is *how* the additional income enters the world.

A daily stipend feels gamey because money appears from nowhere. Real dog people don’t receive a paycheck for existing. The strongest long-term economies usually reward:

* activity
* reputation
* success
* specialization
* risk
* services rendered to other players

That fits your simulation philosophy much better.

For ShowRingGame specifically, I would strongly recommend shifting players from:

> “I own dogs”

to:

> “I operate a kennel business.”

That opens many believable revenue streams.

# Best Income Sources for This Type of Game

## 1. Boarding / Conditioning Services

One of the most realistic systems.

Players can board dogs for:

* conditioning
* coat maintenance
* show prep
* puppy raising
* campaign training

Example:

* A newer player pays another kennel $300/week to condition a special.
* Top handler kennels become businesses.

This is *extremely* real-world accurate.

It also creates:

* prestige economies
* service specialization
* player interdependence

And importantly:
money transfers between players instead of being generated infinitely.

---

## 2. Professional Handlers

You already mentioned handlers — this is a huge one.

A player with:

* strong reputation
* successful campaigns
* high rankings

could become a “professional handler kennel.”

They earn:

* handling fees
* campaign percentages
* bonuses for wins
* travel reimbursements

This creates:

* wealthy but dog-light players
* owner-handlers
* campaign specialists
* realistic social hierarchy

Very authentic to conformation culture.

---

## 3. Grooming / Presentation Services

Especially valuable if coat/conditioning matters.

Examples:

* coat trimming
* presentation prep
* ring handling lessons
* stacking training
* gait training

Even if simplified mechanically, psychologically it makes the world feel alive.

---

## 4. Breeding Consulting / Matchmaking

Advanced players could offer:

* pedigree analysis
* breeding recommendations
* AI scoring projections
* COI balancing

Even if partially system-generated.

This creates “expert breeder” identity.

---

## 5. Hosting Shows

This is one of the best controlled money faucets.

Clubs or kennels could host:

* supported entries
* specialties
* matches
* fun shows
* training matches

Revenue:

* entry fees
* sponsorships
* vendor cuts

Costs:

* judge fees
* venue rental
* ribbons
* staffing

This becomes a massive late-game sink/source system.

---

## 6. Sponsorships / Brand Deals

This is realistic for successful dogs.

High-ranked dogs could receive:

* food sponsorships
* grooming sponsorships
* equipment sponsorships

Not huge money.
More like:

* reduced expenses
* free entries
* travel stipends

This keeps it believable.

Example:

> “Royal Canin has offered partial sponsorship for your Top 10 ranked dog.”

Very immersive.

---

## 7. Seminars / Clinics

Prestige kennels could teach:

* handling classes
* puppy evaluation
* grooming clinics
* breed seminars

This creates:

* educational prestige
* passive-ish income
* community interaction

---

## 8. Judge Licensing

Long-term elder gameplay.

Retired breeder-judges become:

* group judges
* specialty judges
* BIS judges

Then the system can hire them for shows.

This is extremely good for:

* aging player engagement
* prestige progression
* legacy gameplay

Good economies:

# The Safer Version

Instead of:

## Flat permanent bonuses

use:

# Skill-Based Variance Reduction

This is MUCH more sustainable.

---

# Example

A novice handler:

* may overperform sometimes
* may underperform badly

An elite handler:

* is more consistent
* makes fewer mistakes
* presents closer to the dog’s “true quality”

That mirrors real life perfectly.

---

# Mechanically

Instead of:

> +1 score

Do:

* reduced negative variance
* slightly improved category conversion
* improved recovery from bad rolls

Example:

Novice:

```ts
variance = random(-2.0, +2.0)
```

Elite:

```ts
variance = random(-0.8, +1.5)
```

Now elite handlers:

* still lose sometimes
* still need great dogs
* cannot brute-force mediocre dogs to BIS

BUT:
they consistently maximize strong dogs.

That is realistic *and* sustainable.

# Your XP Idea *Can* Work If You Use Soft Curves

This is critical.

Avoid:

```text
100 pts = +1
200 pts = +2
300 pts = +3
```

That explodes.

Instead use:

# Diminishing returns curves.

Example:

| XP    | Effect          |
| ----- | --------------- |
| 0     | 0%              |
| 100   | small           |
| 500   | moderate        |
| 2000  | strong          |
| 10000 | barely stronger |

That prevents ancient players from becoming untouchable gods.
