# Frequently Asked Questions

## 🐾 What is ShowRing?

ShowRing is a realistic dog show and breeding simulation game where you build and manage your own kennel.

You’ll:

* Purchase foundation dogs
* Breed future generations
* Campaign dogs in shows
* Build bloodlines
* Manage conditioning and training
* Grow your kennel reputation

Every dog is unique, and long-term strategy matters.

---

# ⏰ Time & Aging

## How does time work?

In ShowRing:

* **1 real-life hour = 1 in-game day**
* **7 real-life hours = 1 in-game week**
* **365 real-life hours = 1 in-game year**

The world continues moving even while you are offline.

---

## How old does a dog need to be to show?

Dogs become show eligible at approximately:

* **6 months old** (in-game age)

---

## How old does a dog need to be to breed?

Dogs become breed eligible at approximately:

* **2 years old** (in-game age)

Female dogs eventually age out of breeding, while males may continue breeding later in life.

---

## Do dogs age and die?

Yes.

Dogs naturally age over time and eventually retire from showing. Older dogs also begin facing age-related mortality risk.

Part of building a successful kennel is planning for future generations.

---

# 🐶 Dogs & Genetics

## Are dog stats random?

Every dog has hidden inherited traits that influence:

* Movement
* Structure
* Temperament
* Coat quality
* Ring presence
* And more

Puppies inherit traits from both parents, but there is always some variation. Even outstanding parents can produce average puppies — and occasionally exceptional ones.

---

## Can I see hidden genetics?

No.

Players see visible show categories instead of exact genetic values.

This means breeding remains:

* strategic
* realistic
* unpredictable

Learning your bloodlines over time is part of the game.

---

## Why do some dogs win under one judge and lose under another?

Judges do not all value the same things equally.

Some judges may prefer:

* movement
* breed type
* structure
* presentation
* ring attitude

A dog that dominates one weekend may not dominate the next.

---

# 🏠 Kennels

## How do I start my kennel?

New players begin by purchasing foundation dogs from the marketplace.

Foundation dogs are designed to give players a starting point for building their own breeding program.

---

## Are foundation dogs all the same quality?

No.

Some may have:

* useful near-ideal areas
* distinctive profiles
* balanced traits
* traits that pair well with another dog

Others may need careful balancing.

Finding promising dogs is part of the fun.

---

## What makes a successful kennel?

Successful kennels usually focus on:

* smart breeding decisions
* long-term planning
* strong conditioning
* financial management
* selective campaigning

Building a recognizable bloodline takes time.

---

# ❤️ Breeding

## How does breeding work?

To produce a litter:

1. Select an eligible sire and dam
2. Submit a breeding attempt
3. Wait for the pregnancy check
4. If successful, wait for gestation
5. Puppies are born into your kennel

---

## How long does pregnancy take?

Current timing:

* Pregnancy check: approximately 30 game-days after breeding
* Gestation: approximately 60 game-days total

Since 1 real hour = 1 game day:

* Pregnancy checks occur roughly 30 real hours later
* Puppies arrive roughly 60 real hours later

---

## Are litter sizes always the same?

No.

Litters vary naturally in size, just like in real life.

Some litters may be small, while others may be very large.

---

## Can I breed related dogs?

The game allows different breeding strategies, but close breedings may carry risks and consequences over time.

Bloodline management is an important part of advanced kennel strategy.

---

# 🏆 Shows & Competition

## How do shows work?

You enter eligible dogs into available shows during scheduled show weekends.

Dogs are evaluated by judges across multiple categories, including:

* Type & Expression
* Structure & Balance
* Movement
* Coat & Presentation
* Temperament & Ring Behavior
* Conditioning & Handling

---

## Can one dog dominate forever?

No.

Several systems naturally prevent permanent dominance:

* aging
* judge preferences
* conditioning requirements
* breeding variation
* competition depth

Even legendary dogs eventually retire.

---

## Are shows the best way to make money?

Not usually.

Shows are primarily about:

* prestige
* rankings
* reputation
* proving breeding stock

Most kennel income comes from:

* puppy sales
* stud services
* dog sales

---

# 💪 Conditioning & Training

## How does training work?

Each kennel receives a limited number of training sessions per day.

Training improves:

* coat condition
* muscle tone
* show obedience

Conditioning directly affects ring performance.

---

## Can I train every dog every day?

Not fully.

Training sessions are limited, so players must decide:

* which dogs to prioritize
* which campaigns matter most
* where resources are best spent

---

# 💰 Economy

## Why is money important?

Running a kennel is expensive.

Costs can include:

* show entries
* travel
* handlers
* breeding expenses
* kennel upkeep

Managing finances is a major part of long-term success.

---

## What is the best beginner strategy?

New players usually do best by:

* focusing on one breed first
* learning how visible categories sit under, near, or over ideal
* breeding thoughtfully
* conditioning consistently
* campaigning selectively

Strong kennels are built over generations, not overnight.

---

# 📋 Quick Beginner Tips

✅ Start with a small number of dogs
✅ Learn what judges reward
✅ Pay attention to whether visible categories are under ideal, near 10 ideal, or over ideal
✅ Condition dogs regularly
✅ Breed for balance, not perfection
✅ Plan for future generations
